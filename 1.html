Symptoms of Pneumonia

  • Coughing: Often producing phlegm (mucus)
  • Fever: Sweating, shaking chills
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain: Worse when breathing or coughing
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Confusion: Especially in older adults

Precautions to Prevent Pneumonia

  • Get vaccinated: Vaccines are available to prevent some types of pneumonia, including the pneumococcal vaccine and the flu vaccine.
  • Practice good hand hygiene: Wash your hands regularly with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially before eating or touching your face.
  • Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke: Smoking damages the lungs and increases the risk of respiratory infections, including pneumonia.
  • Stay healthy: Eat a balanced diet, exercise regularly, get enough sleep, and manage stress to support a healthy immune system.
  • Avoid close contact with sick individuals: If someone you know has symptoms of a respiratory infection, try to avoid close contact until they recover.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently-touched surfaces: Regularly clean and disinfect surfaces like doorknobs, light switches, and electronic devices to prevent the spread of germs.

Treatment of Pneumonia

  • Antibiotics: If your pneumonia is bacterial, antibiotics can help fight the infection.
  • Antiviral medications: For viral pneumonia, antiviral drugs may be prescribed.
  • Fever reducers and pain relievers: Over-the-counter medications like acetaminophen or ibuprofen can help reduce fever and alleviate discomfort.
  • Rest and hydration: Get plenty of rest and drink fluids to stay hydrated while your body fights the infection.
  • Hospitalization: In severe cases of pneumonia, hospitalization may be necessary, especially for older adults or individuals with underlying health conditions.
